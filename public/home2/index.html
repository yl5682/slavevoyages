Explore the movement of slaves around the Atlantic world

Over 12.5 million Africans were forcibly embarked for the Americas between the sixteenth and nineteenth centuries. Survivors were often subjected to a grueling second journey upon arrival. Drawing on the extensive records left by the largest forced movement of people in global history, slavevoyages provides details of 36,000 transatlantic, and 10,000 intra-American slave voyages as well as the names of 92,000 African captives who were on board them.
