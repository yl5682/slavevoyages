Explore the movement of slaves around the Atlantic world

Over 12.5 million Africans were forcibly embarked for the Americas between the sixteenth and nineteenth centuries – the largest coerced movement of people in global history. Many thousands of the survivors experienced grueling additional journeys within the Americas. Drawing on extensive archival records, the three databases below provide details of 36,000 Trans-Atlantic and 10,000 Intra-American slave voyages, the names and personal details 92,000 captives in the slave trade.

Trans-Atlantic Slave Trades
about | database | estimates | downloads | contribute
The Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade Database has information on almost 36,000 slaving voyages that forcibly embarked more than 12 million Africans for transportation to the Americas between the sixteenth and nineteenth centuries. Drawing from port records, merchant accounts, newspapers, and other primary sources, the database compiles information about slave trading routes, demographics, owners of ships, and enslaved people’s resistance. The database and a separate estimates interface offer scholarly researchers, students, and the general public a chance to discover the reality of the largest forced migration in world history.
Intra-American Slave Trades
about | database | downloads | contribute
The Intra-American Slave Trade Database contains information on approximately 10,000 slave voyages within the Americas. These voyages operated within colonial empires, across imperial boundaries, and inside the borders of nations such as the United States and Brazil. The database enables users to explore the contours of this enormous New World slave trade, which not only dispersed African survivors of the Atlantic crossing but also displaced enslaved people born in the Americas.
People of Trans-Atlantic Slave Trades
about | database | downloads | contribute
People of the Atlantic Slave Trade provides personal details of 91,491 Africans taken from captured slave ships or from African trading sites. It displays the African name, age, gender, origin, country, and places of embarkation and disembarkation of each individual.
